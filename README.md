# XEM7310 FPGA Breakout Board

An Altium Designer hardware project for a custom breakout / interface board for the
[Opal Kelly XEM7310](https://opalkelly.com/products/xem7310/) FPGA module, developed at the
**Berkeley Wireless Research Center (BWRC)**.

The board fans the XEM7310's two high-density mezzanine connectors out to a chip-under-test
("main board") over a Samtec FFSD ribbon cable, generates the bank I/O supplies the test
setup requires, and provides the level shifting, clock-probe, debug, and I²C plumbing needed
to bring up and characterize the device. The main/DUT board on the other end of the cable
lives in the
[pcb-for-128GSPS-adc-based-serdes](https://github.com/kunmok/pcb-for-128GSPS-adc-based-serdes)
repo.

📄 **The full schematic drawing is in [`FPGA_breakout_board.pdf`](FPGA_breakout_board.pdf).**

> is used. Read [Power Architecture & Sequencing](#power-architecture--sequencing) before
> applying power.

## What this board does

- **Mates with the XEM7310** through two Samtec BTE-040 board-to-board connectors (`MC1`, `MC2`).
- **Routes scan-chain, analog, and digital signals** to the main/DUT board through a Samtec
  FFSD ribbon cable (`J_ESHF`), with controlled-impedance series termination.
- **Generates the two FPGA bank I/O supplies** locally:
  - `MC2` bank → **1.2 V** (IOs configured as **LVCMOS12**)
  - `MC1` bank → **3.3 V** (LVCMOS33)
- **Level-shifts** the optional clock-test quad (`CLKQUAD`) scan-chain and the XEM7310
  power-good (`PG_5V5`) flag.
- **Breaks out probe, debug, and control interfaces**: SMA clock/data probes, JTAG for ILA,
  and an I²C interface (Qwiic + 2.54 mm headers, with a USB-to-I²C bridge option).

## Bank I/O supplies

| FPGA bank | Connector | I/O standard | Supply | Generated by |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|--------|--------------|
| `MC2`     | BTE-040   | **LVCMOS12** | **1.2 V** | TPS7A5701 LDO (`U5`), adjustable |
| `MC1`     | BTE-040   | LVCMOS33     | **3.3 V** | TLV1117LV33 LDO (`U1`) |

**Why 3.3 V on `MC1`:** the on-chip **LT3074** regulator's enable pin requires a 3.3 V enable
signal, so the `MC1` bank is run at 3.3 V (LVCMOS33) to drive it directly.

The 1.2 V rail is adjustable via potentiometer `P1V2_POT` (Bourns 3266W, 50 kΩ). Per the
schematic notes, set it before connecting the bank:
- Set step #1 (jumper): ~2.3 V over a 0–2.9 V range
- Set step #2 (jumper): ~1.2 V over a 1–3.0 V range

## Power architecture & sequencing

Input power comes from a 5 V source via the DC barrel jack (`J_JACK`), banana jacks
(`VDCIN_CONN` / `GND_CONN`), or the on-board VBUS option, protected by a TVS diode
(`D3`, SMAJ6.0CA).

> ⚠️ **External 1.2 V supply — required sequence for correct operation.** If the XEM7310's
> bank supply is driven externally through this board, follow the power-up order documented on
> the schematic:
>
> **+5 V (VDCIN) → +1.2 V (VCCO_MC2) → +3.3 V (VCCO_MC1 / MC1)**
>
> Before supplying 1.2 V to `VCCO_MC2`, the corresponding supply path on the XEM7310 module
> must be isolated (remove the relevant ferrite bead / **do not touch FB8** per the schematic
> note) to avoid back-driving the module. This ordering is not about preventing damage — it is
> required for the board and FPGA to power up and function correctly.

### `PG_5V5` power-good flag — two-step level shifting

The XEM7310 `PG_5V5` power-good flag is brought across the supply-voltage boundary using
**two-step level shifting** built from two **SN74LV1T34** single-channel buffers
(`U_LS1`, `U_LS2`). This staging is what makes the careful power-up sequence above necessary
when an external 1.2 V supply is used.

## Clock-test quad (`CLKQUAD`) — optional scan chain

`CLKQUAD` is an **optional** scan-chain control interface (header `J_CLKQUAD`, Samtec STMM-107)
used for clock testing. In practice it is used **only** to measure **CLK jitter** and **ILRO
locking status**, and is otherwise kept **off**.

Because high-speed scan throughput is not needed for this quad, it does **not** consume FPGA
LVCMOS I/O. Instead it is driven through a dedicated **SN74AXC8T245** 8-bit bidirectional
level translator (`U2`), keeping the LVCMOS banks free for the primary signal path.

Clock and data are routed to two **SMA** end-launch connectors
(`J_MEM_CLK`, `J_MEM_DATA`, Bel Cinch 142-0761-881, 26.5 GHz rated) over **50 Ω
characteristic-impedance microstrip**:

- **`MEM_CLK`** (`J_MEM_CLK`) — **100 MHz** clock **generated by the FPGA** and sent **to** the
  [pcb-for-128GSPS-adc-based-serdes](https://github.com/kunmok/pcb-for-128GSPS-adc-based-serdes)
  board.
- **`MEM_DATA_I`** (`J_MEM_DATA`) — **100 Mbps** data **coming from** the
  [pcb-for-128GSPS-adc-based-serdes](https://github.com/kunmok/pcb-for-128GSPS-adc-based-serdes)
  board; primarily carries **retimed ADC output data** to be stored. This link has been
  **successfully tested up to 200 Mbps**; it may support higher rates, but anything beyond
  200 Mbps was not tested as it was unnecessary for this setup.

## Signal integrity — FFSD ribbon cable

Signals to the main board travel over a Samtec FFSD ribbon cable (`J_ESHF`,
ESHF-125-01-L-D-SM). The other end of this cable connects to the main/DUT board in the
[**pcb-for-128GSPS-adc-based-serdes**](https://github.com/kunmok/pcb-for-128GSPS-adc-based-serdes)
repo. The cable's characteristic impedance was **measured empirically in the lab at ≈100 Ω**.

To match it, **100 Ω series-termination resistors** (`R4`–`R31`, Panasonic ERJ-2RKF1000X,
0402) are placed **close to the connector** on the driver side.

## Board stack-up

4-layer PCB:

| Layer | Gerber | Role |
|-------|--------|------|
| Top | `.GTL` | Signal |
| Inner 1 | `.GP1` | Power/ground plane |
| Inner 2 | `.GP2` | Power/ground plane |
| Bottom | `.GBL` | Signal |

The design passes DRC with **0 violations** (see `Design Rule Check - FPGA_breakout_board_v1.drc`).

## Tools & fabrication

- **EDA tool:** Altium Designer. Open `FPGA_breakout_board.PrjPcb` to load the schematic, PCB,
  libraries, and output job.
- **Fabrication & assembly:** this PCB is **fabricated and assembled by
  [JLCPCB](https://jlcpcb.com/)**. Ready-to-send Gerbers, NC drill, BOM, and pick-and-place
  files are in `Project Outputs for FPGA_breakout_board/`. The zipped Gerbers (`Gerber.zip`)
  can be sent directly to a fab house for the 4-layer stack-up above.
- To regenerate manufacturing outputs from source, run the `FPGA_breakout_board.OutJob`.
